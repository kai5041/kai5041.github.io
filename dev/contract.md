## 0. Definitions

“Agreement” means this contract including all sections and appendices.

“Provider” means the service provider stated in this Agreement.

“Client” means the individual or entity entering this Agreement.

“Delivery” means the moment the final agreed work is transferred to the Client after full payment has been received.

“Preview” means any non-final version of the work shared for review purposes and does not constitute delivery or acceptance.

“In writing” means communication recorded in a durable written form, including email and formally executed written documents. Informal messaging platforms are not considered written agreement unless explicitly confirmed via email or signed document.

“Full payment” means the total agreed price received in cleared funds or confirmed cryptocurrency transaction.

“Commencement” means the start of active development after receipt of full payment.

## 8. Termination

Either Party may terminate the Agreement prior to commencement without obligation.

If termination occurs after commencement, the Client shall pay for all work completed up to termination, calculated reasonably based on effort and progress.

Upon termination, any partial deliverables remain the property of the Provider unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Termination does not affect rights or obligations already accrued.

The Provider may terminate the Agreement due to non-cooperation, incorrect information, or legal/compliance restrictions.

## 10. Intellectual Property & Usage Rights

Upon full payment, the Client receives ownership of the final delivered project as defined in the Scope of Work, excluding Background IP and third-party components.

“Background IP” refers to pre-existing or independently developed Provider tools, frameworks, templates, and reusable components.

Background IP may include or interface with third-party open-source libraries (e.g., uWebSockets), which remain subject to their respective licenses.

The Client receives a non-exclusive, perpetual right to use Background IP solely as embedded within the delivered project. Background IP may not be extracted, reused independently, or redistributed outside the project context.
